Welcome! I'm a Postdoctoral Education Researcher in the Ecology & Evolutionary Biology Department at UC Santa Cruz, studying the design and impact of experiential teaching & learning in ecology with the "FUTURE" (Field-based Undergraduate Training: Utilizing Research for Equity) in Biology team (NSF #2329523), led by PIs Roxanne Beltran, Robin Dunkin, and Erika Zavaleta.

My postdoctoral work focuses on how students learn and change as a result of participating in undergraduate educational experiences that use experiential learning (like field-based, research-based, and inquiry-based courses) with a particular focus on how course design choices impact student outcomes and educational equity. It connects interdisciplinary themes in education and cognitive psychology on the relationship between learning, memory, and metacognition, and also asks how research-based equity-minded and inclusive teaching practices can support student learning and persistence in the sciences.

In September of 2024, I received my Ph.D. in Linguistics, advised by Matt Wagers, from the UC Santa Cruz Linguistics Department. My dissertation work was in psycholinguistics, focusing on how readers incrementally partition higher-order linguistic units in memory, using a temporal context mechanism during encoding that is sensitive to a sentence's intonational structure, which facilitates more targeted memory retrieval operations during language comprehension in certain structural contexts; you can read it here. In shifting from linguistics to education research, a common thread that ties my past and current work together is broadly understanding the mechanisms that drive learning & comprehension.
